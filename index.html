Knowledge & Engineering Insights

This section shares practical injection mold design insights based on real project experience. The focus is on engineering decisions that directly influence tool performance, part quality, and first-trial success.

1. Part Geometry & Wall Thickness

Evaluating part geometry early helps identify thin walls, deep ribs, sharp corners, and tolerance-critical areas. Maintaining uniform wall thickness improves flow consistency and minimizes sink marks and warpage.

2. Gate & Runner Design

Gate location and runner layout directly impact filling balance, weld lines, and cosmetic quality. Gate type selection depends on part geometry, material behavior, and production volume.

3. Cooling System Design

Cooling efficiency determines cycle time and dimensional stability. Cooling channels must be positioned to maintain uniform mold temperature and eliminate localized hot spots.

4. Draft Angles & Ejection

Proper draft angles reduce ejection force and prevent part damage. Ejector layout should support smooth part release without deformation or stress marks.

5. Mold Flow Analysis

Mold flow analysis predicts filling behavior, pressure demand, and potential defects. It supports informed design decisions and improves first-trial tool performance.
